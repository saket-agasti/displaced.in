Displaced Indica

A project on conservation-induced displacement in Indian tiger reserves

About the Project

Displaced Indica is a knowledge-building and community-driven initiative focused on the ecological and social dimensions of village relocation from India's protected areas. The project highlights the historical context, policy landscape, spatial mapping, and lived experiences of displaced communities and the wildlife they share space with.

Objectives

  • Document the history and evolution of resettlement from Indian tiger reserves
  • Map village relocations across India using interactive tools
  • Understand the socio-economic outcomes for relocated communities
  • Support academic and field-based research on conservation displacement
  • Build a collaborative platform for practitioners, scholars, and local voices
